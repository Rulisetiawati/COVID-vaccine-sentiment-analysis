Project Workflow

Near the end of 2020, we noticed that many people became skeptical about the vaccines from Pfizer, Moderna, Astrazeneca, and Sinovac. Online discussions always seemed so one-sided, with many people misinformed about the vaccine. In addition, we saw that many anti-vaccine groups grew online, and we wanted to explore the trends associated with this. So, we decided to build a model that could aggregate tweets and analyze the sentiment trends.

Data Exploration

After scraping the data and annotating 10,000+ tweets, we tried three approaches:

Supervised Learning:
  • Linear models: Logistic Regression
  • Non-linear Models: Naive Bayes, SVM, Decision Trees
  • Ensembles: Random Forest, LGBM, CatBoost
    • Unsupervised Learning:
  • K-means
  • SVD + Topic Modelling
  • Genism
    • Deep Learning Models:
  • Baseline: RNN
  • More complex sequence models: GRU, LSTM (bidirectional)
  • Integrated GloVe Embedding to improve performance
    • After hyperparamter tuning, our model had the least overfitting/underfitting, and also had the highest validation score of 78.78%

    Data Visualization

    Our dataset is trained on tweets from the last week of December 2020, and we noticed that the tweets contained many headlines from news outlets. So, we decided to label those as "Nuetral". As shown in the graph, we had a large imbalanced dataset to deal with, since there were many auto-generated headline tweets, and not as many negative and positive tweets from real users.
    Being able to explain data visually is very important to us, since explaining data to other team members and stakeholders is an important part of being a good Data Scientist.
